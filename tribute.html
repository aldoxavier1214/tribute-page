Kumaraswami Kamaraj

15 July 1903 - 2 October 1975

Kumaraswami Kamaraj popularly known as Kamarajar was an Indian independence activist and politician
As a young boy, Kamaraj worked in his uncle's provision shop and during that time he began to attend the Indian Home Rule movement.
As Chief Minister, Kamaraj removed the family vocation based Modified Scheme of Elementary education 1953 introduced by Rajaji.
Kamaraj remained Chief Minister for three consecutive terms, winning elections in 1957 and 1962.
After Nehru's death in 1964, Kamaraj successfully navigated the party through turbulent times.
Kamaraj noticed that the Congress party was slowly losing its vigour.
During his tenure as Chief Minister, when the municipality of Virudhunagar provided a direct water connection to his house in his hometown
Kamarajar ordered it to be disconnected immediately as he did not want any special privileges
He served as the Chief Minister of Madras State (Tamil Nadu) from 13 April 1954 to 2 October 1963
He was the founder and the president of the Indian National Congress widely acknowledged as the "Kingmaker"

He was responsible for the elevation of Lal Bahadur Shastri to the position of Prime Minister of India after Jawaharlal Nehru's death.

He was aged 72 and died in his sleep due to a heart attack.
